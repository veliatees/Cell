# Epithelial Cell Starting Scope

## Why Epithelial Cells

Epithelial cells are a strong first cell target because they are physical
boundary-makers. They form sheets, separate compartments, transport material,
respond to signals, and connect strongly to neighboring cells and extracellular
matrix.

## Core Features

- apical surface facing lumen or outside-like compartment
- basolateral surface facing tissue/interstitial compartment
- tight junctions controlling paracellular permeability
- adherens junctions linking neighboring cells through the cytoskeleton
- desmosomes and hemidesmosomes for mechanical stability in many epithelial
  contexts
- basal lamina / extracellular matrix contact
- polarized membrane protein distribution

## Transport Routes

### Transcellular

Material crosses through the cell, using apical and basolateral transporters,
channels, pumps, vesicles, or diffusion.

### Paracellular

Material passes between neighboring cells. Tight junction composition and state
control how permissive this route is.

## Initial Inputs

- oxygen
- water
- glucose
- amino acids
- fatty acids
- sodium, potassium, calcium, chloride, hydrogen, bicarbonate
- hormones and cytokines
- neurotransmitter-like signals where tissue-appropriate
- mechanical force
- electrical gradients
- extracellular matrix cues

## Initial Outputs

- carbon dioxide
- water
- lactate depending on metabolic state
- secreted proteins
- ions
- heat
- vesicles
- signaling molecules
- waste handled by lysosomal/autophagy and metabolic pathways
- mechanical force on neighbors and extracellular matrix

## Model Variables

- membrane potential
- intracellular pH
- ATP/ADP/AMP state
- redox state
- ion concentrations
- osmotic pressure
- volume
- cytoskeletal tension
- junction permeability
- apical and basolateral transporter inventory
- mitochondrial state
- stress state

## Source-Backed Starting Claims

- Epithelial sheets line cavities and free surfaces of the body and use
  specialized junctions to form barriers between compartments.
- Tight junctions form selective permeability barriers across epithelial sheets.
- Epithelial transport can occur through cells or between cells, depending on
  transporter placement and junction permeability.

## Open Questions

- Which epithelial subtype should be first: intestinal, airway, kidney tubule,
  skin/keratinocyte-like, or generic polarized epithelium?
- Should the first epithelial model include one cell or a small sheet of cells?
- Should the first transport demo be glucose uptake, sodium/potassium gradients,
  water/osmosis, or calcium signaling?
